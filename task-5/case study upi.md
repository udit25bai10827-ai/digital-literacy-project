CASE STUDY- UPI



Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud is a type of cybercrime where fraudsters trick users into transferring money or gaining unauthorized access to their bank accounts. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such frauds have become increasingly common.

Typically, UPI fraud follows a step-by-step pattern. First, the fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media, often pretending to be a bank official, customer care agent, or even a police officer. Second, they create a sense of urgency or fear, such as claiming an account will be blocked or offering a fake reward. Third, the victim is asked to click on a phishing link, scan a QR code, or approve a “collect request.” In some cases, they are tricked into sharing sensitive details like OTPs or UPI PINs. Finally, once access is gained or authorization is given, the fraudster transfers money from the victim’s account instantly.

The usual targets are individuals with limited digital awareness, elderly people, and first-time users of online banking. Small business owners and online shoppers are also frequent victims due to their regular use of UPI transactions.

The consequences of UPI fraud can be severe. Victims often suffer financial losses ranging from a few thousand to several lakhs of rupees. Additionally, they may experience stress, anxiety, and a loss of trust in digital payment systems. Recovery of money is often difficult, making prevention and awareness crucial.
