Prevention Checklist


Use strong, unique passwords

Create different passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities

Do not access banking apps or make payments over unsecured public networks in cafés, hostels, or campuses.
Do not click on unknown links
Ignore suspicious links sent via SMS, email, or social media, even if they appear to come from known contacts.

Verify before you trust
Always double-check calls or messages claiming to be from banks, delivery services, or authorities.

UPI Safety Tip: Never share UPI PIN or OTP
No bank or app will ever ask for your PIN or OTP. Sharing it can lead to instant money loss.

UPI Safety Tip: Check ‘Collect Requests’ carefully
Approving a request means you are sending money—not receiving it. Read payment prompts before confirming.

Download apps only from official stores
Install apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store to avoid malware or fake apps.

Log out from shared devices
Always log out from email, banking, and social media accounts when using public or shared computers.

Keep your phone and apps updated
Regular updates fix security vulnerabilities and protect against new threats.

Report fraud immediately
If you face any cyber fraud, report it quickly on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930 to improve chances of recovering your money.
